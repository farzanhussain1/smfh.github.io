LISTENING TO IN 2016

Which bands should you be downloading, streaming or buying tickets to see live this year? Here's our pick of the crop.

1. ALL TVVINS

The Dublin duo of Lar Kaye and Conor Adams already have a wealth of experience between them. Kaye was a member of the trio Adebisi Shank, while Adams was an occasional member of The Cast Of Cheers. Their love of glorious pop showed on their fantastic single Darkest Ocean and they attracted some attention by covering Justin Bieber’s hit as WHERE R U NOVV. Their debut album IIVV drops in August, with a headline tour of the UK due in October.

 

2. THE AMAZONS

 

This Reading quartet - fronted by the powerful vocals of Matt Thomson - have been perfecting their own brand of melodic indie rock for the past year. They achieved something of a buzz with the release of their debut single, Junk Food Forever, in the summer, while the follow-up EP, Don’t You Wanna, arrived at the end of the year. A new single, Nightdriving, came along in June and the band are set to tour in the autumn, with an appearance at Neighbourhood Festival in Manchester on 8 October. 

 

3. BABY STRANGE



Baby Strange are a punk pop trio from Glasgow, and a favourite of Palma Violets and Slaves. Summer 2015 saw the band appear at Mumford And Sons’ Gentlemen Of The Road show in Aviemore, Scotland. Their debut album arrives in September, with a UK tour to follow. 

 

4. BLOSSOMS

With a warm retro sound and killer look, this Stockport band look set to join the growing of number of bands bringing psychedelic music bang up to date. 2015 saw three immense singles to come from the band via James Skelly of The Coral’s Skeleton Key Records and August 2016 saw the release of an accomplished self-titled debut album and a summer of touring. 

 

5.  GEORGE COSBY

The dark voice of George Cosby is already a firm favourite of our very own Maz Tappuni on his Communion Show on Radio X late last year. Hailing from South London, George cites influences as diverse as Jeff Buckley, The National, Eddie Vedder and Bon Iver, and released his debut EP, Human Touch, in November. Summer 2016 has seen George play festivals such as Latitude, and he has a new headline show in London at the Moth Club on 27 September. 

 

6. ESTRONS

 
Hailing from Cardiff in South Wales, Estrons are fronted by Canadian-Swede Taliesyn Källström and guitarist Rhodri Daniel and first got together in 2012 after “a chance encounter on a beach” in Aberystwyth. Their dynamic mix of indie punk was first aired on their single C-C-Cariad at the end of 2014 and their EP, Whoever She Was, arrived in December on Gofod Records and features the superb lead track Make A Man.  The band head out on a UK tour with The Bay Rays in September. 

 

7.  ADAM FRENCH

22-year-old Adam French is from Congleton in Cheshire, with a distinctively smooth vocal style and skills on guitar, bass, piano and drums. His debut EP, More To Life, was issued at the end of 2014 and the past twelve months have seen shows at Bushstock, Latitude and The Great Escape. He's currently recording his debut album and tours the UK in September. 

 

8. JACK GARRATT

This 24-year-old multi-instrumentalist from Buckinghamshire comes into 2016 having already reached huge audiences by supporting Ben Howard and Mumford And Sons over the past twelve months. But he has talent to back up the buzz: his single Weathered was a huge favourite of Radio X’s in 2015 and his much-anticipated debut album Phase arrived in February. After a summer of festivals and a tour of the US, Jack returns for British dates in November. 

 

9. INHEAVEN


This South London quartet produce blissed out sheets of guitar noise that caught the ear of the Strokes frontman Julian Casablancas, who released their debut single, Regeneration, on his Cult Records imprint in the US. A new single, All There Is, arrived in August and September and October sees a full UK tour. 

 

10. LAKE KOMO

Together since 2012, the quartet of Jay, Pete, Maxwell and Jess have been performing some well-received shows in and around their hometowns of Lancaster and Kendal. They released the debut single, Ritual, in May 2015 and in the autumn, the band moved to Manchester to record their debut album, offering an EP, Resurrect, on the major label Columbia. The band release a new track, Milwaukee, in June.

 

11. BILLIE MARTEN


Billie is a 16-year-old singer songwriter from Yorkshire with a delicate, bewitching voice. The As Long As EP was released in November and featured the excellent track Bird, which she describes as “a song about how words can truly affect people, not always for the right reasons”. Her debut album, Writings Of Blues And Yellows, arrives in September and is followed by a string of UK dates. 

12. RUKHSANA MERRISE

Hailing from West London, Rukhsana came to the attention of the world by releasing a song every week in the month of September 2014. The resulting “September Songs” EP clocked up a quarter of a million plays on Soundcloud, causing our pals at Communion Records to sign her up and release the excellent single Money. 

 

13. NIMMO

 

This London-based five-piece are fronted by the charismatic Sarah Nimmo and Reva Gauntlett. The band signed to Columbia in 2014 and have been honing their sophisticated brand of electro pop with shows supporting Years And Years. Their track My Only Friend dropped in June and a UK tour is lined up for October. 

 

14. PRETTY VICIOUS

Brad Griffiths, Thomas McCarthy, Jarvis Morgan and Elliot Jones are all between 17 and 19 years old and are in the hottest new band in the UK right now. Pretty Vicious are from the Welsh town of Merthyr Tydfil and last year had virtually everyone in the British music industry currently trying to sign them - Virgin EMI Records were the lucky ones. With their no-nonsense rock’n’roll style, their debut album will prove to be an instant classic. A UK tour is set for September. 

 

15. TOM PRIOR



A former rapper turned singer-songwriter from Enfield, North London, Tom Prior started making music in his shed back in 2005. Having caught the ear of Alex Turner, his debut EP was recorded with former Arctic Monkeys bassist Andy Nicholson and the result sounds a little like Jake Bugg but with a keyboard instead of a guitar. A new EP, The Sunday Scene, is due any time soon. 

16. RAT BOY



19-year-old Jordan Cardy hails from Chelmford and has come up with a very Essex take on hip hop. Jordan has just finished a support slot with The 1975 on their UK tour and is on the bill for the 2016 Reading and Leeds festivals. He heads out on the road in September in support of the new EP, Get Over It.  

17. STARLING

Originally from Surrey, now based in Soho, Harriet Starling calls herself “a real girl in an unreal world” and her songs deal with “depression, solitude and the darker sides of the human conditions.” Sprinkling her songs with some light synths and the odd electronic beat makes clear her declared influences: Massive Attack, Portishead, Florence and the Machine and more. She plays two London showcases in October. 

 

18. SUNDARA KARMA

Sundara means “beautiful” in Sanskrit, and this quartet live up to their blissful name with some epic and anthemic indie rock, gaining comparisons from Arcade Fire to Bruce Springsteen. And Oscar Lulu (vocals guitar), Ally Baty (guitar), Dom Cordell (bass) and Haydn Evans (drums) are still only teenagers. After the well-received Loveblood single, new material is due in August, with a UK tour to follow in September. 

 

19. LAWRENCE TAYLOR

Just 22 years old, singer-songwriter Lawrence Taylor has a raw, bluesy sound that immediately attracted A&R men across the nation. Compared to artists as diverse as James Bay, Hozier and George Ezra, Taylor’s music is simple, honest and emotional, with a classic rock guitar backing. A summer of touring the US will see Lawrence return to the UK in the autumn with a support slot for Birdy.

 

20. VIOLA BEACH

When we first published this feature back in January 2016, this young band from Warrington were set for great things. They’d released a well-received single, Swings And Waterslides and the band were heading out on a tour of the UK, which included a hometown show in March.

All of this potential was abruptly ended when the entire band - Kris Leonard (guitar, vocals), River Reeves (guitar), Tomas Lowe (bass) and Jack Dakin (drums) - died in a car accident alongside their manager Craig Tarry on 13 February 2016, while travelling back from their first European festival appearance in Sweden.

It was a horribly tragic end to a promising new band at the beginning of their career. Radio X had supported Viola Beach from the beginning and had tipped them for great things - tragically they were not around to see their self-titled debut album reach Number 1 in the UK charts. Such a shame. RIP.
